# Brand Profile Information

This document outlines the information needed to create a complete brand profile. This data powers consistent content generation across podcasts, voiceovers, videos, and other media.

---

## Brand Foundation

### Core Identity

- **Brand Name** - The official name of the brand
- **Tagline / Slogan** - A memorable phrase that captures the brand essence
- **Mission Statement** - Why the brand exists and what problem it solves
- **Core Values** - 3-5 principles that guide decisions and behavior

### Target Audience

- **Ideal Audience** - Demographics, role, situation of the primary audience
- **Problems Solved** - The pain points or challenges the brand addresses
- **Audience Priorities** - What matters most to the audience
- **Content Consumption** - Where and how the audience consumes content (platforms, formats, contexts)

### Content Direction

- **Topics & Themes** - Subject matter the content should cover
- **Off-Limits Topics** - Subjects to avoid
- **Content Pillars** - 3-5 main themes that anchor all content
- **Primary Goal** - The main purpose of content (educate, entertain, convert, build community, establish authority)

### Visual Identity

- **Brand Colors** - Primary, secondary, accent, background, and text colors with hex codes
- **Typography** - Font families for headlines, body text, and accents
- **Visual Style** - The overall aesthetic (minimalist, bold, elegant, playful, professional, edgy, etc.)
- **Logo Variations** - Available versions (full color, single color, dark/light backgrounds, icon only)

## Brand Personas

A persona is a character or voice that represents the brand. A brand may have one primary persona or several for different contexts (e.g., a podcast host, an explainer voice, a social media personality).

### Persona Identity

- **Name** - Real or character name
- **Role / Title** - Their function (e.g., "Host", "Expert Guide", "Friendly Narrator")
- **Bio / Backstory** - Brief background that informs their perspective
- **Personality Traits** - 3-5 adjectives that define their character
- **Audience Relationship** - How they relate to the listener/viewer (mentor, peer, expert, friend, entertainer, guide)

### Voice Characteristics

*Used for podcasts and voiceovers*

- **Voice Gender** - Male, female, non-binary, or no preference
- **Age Range** - Young adult (20s), adult (30s-40s), mature (50s+)
- **Voice Qualities** - Characteristics like warm, authoritative, energetic, calm, conversational, professional, friendly, confident, empathetic
- **Speaking Pace** - Slow & deliberate, moderate, fast & energetic, or context-dependent
- **Accent / Dialect** - Any regional or stylistic speech preferences
- **Speech Patterns** - Signature phrases, verbal habits, or catchphrases

### Visual Representation

*Used for videos, graphics, and avatars*

- **Representation Style** - Real human, illustrated character, 3D animated, abstract/symbolic
- **Appearance** - General look and vibe if human-like
- **Age Appearance** - Visual age range
- **Clothing / Style** - Casual, business casual, professional, creative
- **Defining Traits** - Visual elements that make the persona recognizable

### Usage Context

- **Content Types** - Where this persona appears (podcasts, voiceovers, videos, graphics, articles)
- **Interaction Mode** - Solo, with guests, part of a duo/group, or with other personas
- **Exclusions** - Content types where this persona should not appear

---

## Persona Dynamics

*When a brand has multiple personas*

- **Co-appearance** - Whether personas appear together and how often
- **Dynamic** - The relationship between personas (co-hosts with banter, interview-style, mentor/student, complementary experts)
- **Handoffs** - How different personas are used for different content types

---

## Competitive Context

- **Admired Brands** - Brands (even outside the industry) whose content or voice is aspirational
- **Competitors** - Others speaking to the same audience
- **Differentiators** - What makes this brand unique
